# The fish fry canon: a field guide

Kolkata has opinions about many things. On the fish fry, it has **laws**.

The fish fry — note: not "fried fish", an entirely different and lesser
concept — is a breadcrumbed bhetki fillet that arrived via colonial-era
kitchens, was perfected in the cabins of North Kolkata, and reached its final
form at wedding buffets, where it remains the true measure of a family's
seriousness.

## The laws, as inherited

1. **Bhetki or nothing.** Substituting basa is legal only in the narrow
   technical sense. The para will know. The para always knows.
2. **The crumb must crack.** A proper fish fry announces itself acoustically.
   If your neighbour can't hear the first bite, return it.
3. **Kashundi is a right, not a garnish.** The fermented mustard should be
   within arm's reach and applied with the confidence of a signature.
4. **The shape is a leaf.** Flat, tapered, elegant. Nobody remembers deciding
   this. It is simply so.
5. **Onion rings and a lime wedge** stand witness. They are rarely eaten.
   They are always present.

## Why it survives

Fancier snacks have come and gone — momos included, and we say that with
love. The fish fry survives because it's a **ritual disguised as a cutlet**:
the crack, the mustard sting, the moment of silence.
