# ₹20 economics: the coffee that refuses inflation

Somewhere in this city, a café is charging ₹280 for a coffee with a name
longer than this paragraph. We respect the hustle. We do not participate.

Our Belgian Coffee — thick, chocolatey, whipped into minor luxury — costs
**₹20**. It cost ₹20 when the first steamer hissed. It will cost ₹20 for as
long as arithmetic allows, and possibly a little longer.

## Frequently asked questions, answered honestly

- **"How?"** Volume, stubbornness, and a kettle that has achieved seniority.
- **"Why?"** Because a student should be able to order one, think about it,
  and order another without consulting a budget app.
- **"What's Belgian about it?"** Historians disagree. Nobody has stopped
  drinking long enough to investigate properly.

## The real economics

Here's the secret the spreadsheet people miss: the ₹20 coffee is our
**marketing department, loyalty program and quality bond** in one glass. It
brings people to the counter; the momos and the bhetki do the rest. Every cup
is an advertisement that pays for itself and then orders a fish fry.
